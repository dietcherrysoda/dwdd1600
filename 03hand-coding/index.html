Finding Your Own Personal Style !

Finding your personal style isn’t about following every trend — it’s about discovering what makes you feel confident, comfortable, and true to who you are. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you along the way:

  1. Start With What You Love
  2. Look Inside Your Closet
  3. Experiment
  4. Shop With Intention
  5. Seek Visual Inspiration
  6. Dress For Your Lifestyle
  7. Embrace Your Style Evolution

Wordrobe Essentials

  • A Classic White, Black or any Neurtal Colored Top
  • A Neutral Long Skirt or Midi Skirt
  • A Modest Dress (Everyday + Elevated)
  • A Lightweight Cardigan or Kimono
  • A Structured Jacket or Blazer
  • Basic Layering Tops (Short/Long Sleeve)
  • A Comfortable Pair of Sneakers
  • Flats or any Dress Shoes
  • A Scarf or any Statement Accessory
  • An Everyday Bag
  • Any Demin Piece
  • Any Utility Jacket

Style Tips While Also Being Modesty

  • Balance Fit & Coverage – Choose pieces that are loose but still flattering.
  • Layer Creatively – Add coverage with turtlenecks, cardigans, or button-downs.
  • Experiment with Texture & Color – Mix fabrics and don’t shy away from prints or bold tones.
  • Add Shape with Belts – Define your waist with belts over dresses or tunics.
  • Use Statement Accessories – Elevate simple outfits with scarves, jewelry, or bags.
  • Go Long with Style – Choose maxi skirts or dresses with fun details like pleats or tiers.

Shopping Sustainably

Shopping sustainably means making mindful choices that benefit both the planet and the people behind our clothes. You can start by thrifting, shopping secondhand online, or supporting small and ethical brands that use eco-friendly materials. Buying fewer, better-quality pieces and taking care of what you own can make a big difference. Look for timeless styles, learn to read fabric labels, and try swapping or borrowing instead of always buying new. You can even make your own, by sewing, crochetting or even Knitting your own garnments! Sustainable fashion is all about being intentional — not perfect — and making choices that align with your values and personal style.
